LibreWolf vs Firefox

Quick answer: LibreWolf aims to remove or harden settings in Firefox that could lead to telemetry or data leakage. It keeps core Firefox compatibility but changes defaults. Use LibreWolf if you prefer a privacy-first preset; use official Firefox for the mainstream release cadence, telemetry options, and vendor support.

At a glance

This page compares the unofficial LibreWolf builds with the upstream Mozilla Firefox, focusing on Windows: telemetry, update models, extension compatibility, and release differences. Both are derived from the same codebase, but LibreWolf applies different default configurations.

Why LibreWolf exists

LibreWolf provides a pre-configured, privacy-reduced version of Firefox for users who do not want to manually tweak dozens of preferences. It’s useful for privacy-conscious users, security-focused setups, and contexts where telemetry should be avoided.

Risks & trade-offs

  • Security updates: while LibreWolf tracks upstream fixes, users must ensure they get timely updates — use winget or monitor releases.
  • Compatibility: some upstream features or telemetry-dependent services may be impacted by changed defaults.
  • Support: LibreWolf is community-driven; for enterprise-grade vendor support, prefer official Firefox.

Recommended users

  • Privacy enthusiasts who prefer a pre-hardened browser without telemetry.
  • Users who want Firefox compatibility but with privacy-first defaults.
  • System administrators deploying stricter privacy configurations without manual profile changes.

Migration and verification

If you move from Firefox to LibreWolf, export bookmarks and sync critical data. Verify installer checksums on the Download page and follow update instructions on Install.

Verdict

LibreWolf is a privacy-oriented, pre-hardened build of Firefox. For users who trust community builds and prefer minimal telemetry, LibreWolf is a strong choice. For enterprise support and official provenance, choose Mozilla Firefox.
